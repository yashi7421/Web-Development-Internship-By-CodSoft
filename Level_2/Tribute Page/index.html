Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Sir

1875 - 1950

Iron Man

When a country at the brink of independence was struggling to find its geography of unity, there was one outstanding individual who took upon his shoulders the gigantic task of shaping the edifice of the nation. With his indomitable spirit and relentless efforts, he made it possible to integrate hundreds of remarkably diverse states to form the modern nation-state of India. The name of this visionary- Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.[1] Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31st October 1875 in Nadiad, Gujarat. A successful lawyer by profession, his life encountered a turning point when Mahatma Gandhi chose him as his deputy commander to lead the Kheda Satyagraha in 1918. Thus, as the leader of a peasants’ protest, Vallabhbhai Patel found the trajectory of his life turning towards a path of public service.[2] Vallabhbhai Patel became increasingly involved with the struggle for independence. It was his role in the Bardoli Satyagraha of 1928 that elevated him to a new pinnacle of national glory. The peasant movement that became a subject of great discussion across the nation demonstrated the Sardar organisational capacity and enthusiasm for tireless action. It was here that he earned the title of Sardar, the fond epithet by which he continues to be remembered and revered.



About The Legend

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel served as the 1st Deputy Prime Minister of India from 1947 to 1950.
  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel earned a degree in law from Middle Temple Inn in London and in 1913 returned to India to join the legal profession.
  • In 1917, he became actively involved in the Indian National Congress, where he played a key role in organizing the Kheda Satyagraha, a successful nonviolent movement that helped farmers in Gujarat resist oppressive tax policies imposed by the British.
  • Rejoining the Indian National Congress in 1928, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel led the Bardoli Satyagraha, a successful farmers' revolt against increased taxes, which earned him the nickname "Sardar," meaning leader or chief. “Iron Man.”
  • Among those successes was the integration of the princely states into the Indian Union, where Patel's diplomatic efforts and use of force when necessary led to the peaceful unification of over 560 states by 1949.
  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel received 7 numerous honors for his contributions to 40 India's freedom struggle. The Government of India posthumously awarded him the Bharat Ratna in 1991 and the country's highest civilian honor in recognition of his role in uniting India and his leadership during the independence movement.
  • In 1991, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was posthumously awarded India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, for his pivotal role in the integration of India and his leadership in the independence movement. In recognition of his legacy, the Statue of Unity, the world’s tallest statue, was unveiled in 2018 to honor his contributions to the nation.
  • A prominent airport in Ahmedabad was renamed Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in honor of his contributions to India’s unity and independenceAdditionally, several institutions and landmarks across the country, including Sardar Patel Marg in New Delhi, bear his name.
  • In 2013, a newly discovered species of rose was named Rosa 'Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel' in honor of his enduring legacy as the "Iron Man of India" and his contributions to the nation’s unity and independence.
